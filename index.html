Mughal Empire: A Chronicle of Cruelty, Courage & Concealed Truths

Expose the whitewashed legacy of the Mughals, from Babur’s butchery to Aurangzeb’s temple razing. Reclaim Bharat’s erased memory — from the ashes of Nalanda to the blood-stained pages of NCERT.

📌 Project Overview

This project is not just a historical handout — it is a civilizational weapon. Born from outrage at NCERT’s glorification of tyrants, this work documents the concealed brutality of the Mughal Empire, the genocide of temples, the targeted erasure of Brahmin knowledge systems, and the dharmic resistance that fought with swords and shlokas.

From the burning of Nalanda and Takshashila to the whitewashing of Akbar’s Meena Bazaar and Aurangzeb’s Kashi raids, this chronicle revives suppressed truths. It combines primary native sources, eyewitness accounts, and decolonial analysis to break the mental chains of fake history imposed by colonial and Marxist filters.
